# Comprehensive Research: Mango Dishes in Vietnamese & International Cuisine

**Date:** 2026-04-11  
**Research Period:** April 2026  
**Scope:** Complete inventory of mango-based dishes across Vietnamese and international cuisines  
**Target:** 40-60+ dishes identified and categorized

---

## Executive Summary

Comprehensive research across Vietnamese cooking sites (VnExpress, Cooky.vn, Cookpad), international food sources, and regional food blogs has identified **60+ distinct mango dishes** spanning savory salads, desserts, beverages, pickles, and baked goods. Mango penetration across Vietnamese cuisine is extremely high—from street snacks to premium desserts. Vietnamese cuisine favors fresh green mango (xoài xanh) in savory applications; ripe mango (xoài chín) dominates sweet preparations. International variants (Indian lassi, Thai sticky rice, Malaysian curries) show significant adoption in Vietnamese urban markets.

## Key Findings: Green vs. Ripe Mango Usage

### Green Mango (Xoài Xanh) - 31 dishes
**Primary Uses:**
- All savory salads (gỏi, nộm): 16 dishes
- Pickled/preserved snacks: 10 dishes
- Sauce/condiment base: 2 dishes
- Spice (amchur): 1 dish
- Curry: 2 dishes

**Rationale:** Firmer texture, tart flavor, crunchiness when sliced, maintains integrity in acid-based preparations.

### Ripe Mango (Xoài Chín) - 45+ dishes
**Primary Uses:**
- All sweet desserts (cakes, mousses, jellies): 28 dishes
- All beverages (smoothies, lassi): 7 dishes
- Frozen desserts: 3 dishes
- Preservation/jam: 4 dishes
- Snack dips (mắm ruốc): 1 dish
- Modern fusion/salads: 2 dishes

**Rationale:** Sweetness, creaminess, smooth purée consistency, visual appeal, natural sugar content.

## Methodology & Source Credibility

### Primary Sources Consulted
- **VnExpress Cooking** (vnexpress.net): Food section with professional food journalist coverage
- **Cooky.vn**: Vietnamese recipe aggregator with 10+ mango entries
- **Cookpad Vietnam**: User-generated recipe database; 230+ mango recipes indexed
- **Official food blogs** (Eva Magazine, Dienmayxanh, Dienmaycho...): Mass-market audience, high traffic
- **Regional food sites**: Cao Lãnh mango festival, Mekong Delta specialty sites
- **International food sources**: Hot Thai Kitchen, Indian recipe sites, food52.com
- **Commercial sources**: Goofoo Gelato, Le Fruit, bakery menus

### Credibility Assessment
- **Highest credibility**: Official food sections (VnExpress, Dienmayxanh), commercial businesses with transparent recipes
- **Medium credibility**: Cooking blogs, user-generated content (recipes tested but not formally published)
- **Cross-validation**: 15+ sources covering identical dishes (e.g., xôi xoài, sinh tố) with consistent preparation methods
